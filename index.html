Media Design Studio

Media Design Studio is an advanced media design portfolio course that combines seminar and critique with studio work. We will review each other’s work and work to develop our sense of curiousity and creativity.

The ultimate goal of the course is for students to produce a hand-built portfolio of designed artifacts. Previously designed and printed pieces or work for other classes can be included in the final portfolio, but may not be submitted for credit.

At least one significant project must be completed during the course. This is an intense course with advanced students working on different media design and interactive design problems. A basic understanding of technology, applications, and design theory is expected.

Class sessions will be utilized for demos, instructor/peer critiques, reading discussion and for solving technical production problems.

Course objectives

Identify examples of work from professional designers and developers that you find inspirational

Write a project plan for one or more significant projects

Produce one or more significant projects that expand your understanding of graphic and/or interactive design

Develop an online portfolio that hosts a collection of your original work

Present works-in-progress and completed work to your peers and instructor for in-class critiques

Idea objectives

Learning to apply course material (to improve thinking, problem solving, and decisions)

Developing specific skills, competencies, and points of view needed by professionals in the field most closely related to this course

Developing creative capacities (inventing, designing, writing, performing in art, music, drama, etc.)

Developing skill in expressing oneself orally or in writing

Learning how to find, evaluate, and use resources to explore a topic in depth

My mission statement

Good design is universal and great design is personal. As a journalist, I believe the most powerful design serves the audience and conveys information in the clearest and most powerful medium for the content. I believe creativity, inspiration and motivation are not abstract ideas but skills we can develop through practice and curiosity.

Attendance

An effective classroom experience depends on everyone involved: faculty and students alike. Therefore, attending class is required in all Emerging Media courses. In this class student grades will be reduced by ½ a grade point once more than three absences occur (A to A-). Another ½ grade point reduction will be applied for each additional absence. Any absences will also cause participation points (10% of the total points) to be reduced potentially lowering the final grade.

Deadlines

Deadlines for class projects must be met to receive the points earned. Late work will only be accepted as a result of documented medical emergencies experienced by the student or an immediate family member.

Respectful behavior

The best learning takes place in an atmosphere of respect. Therefore, students should arrive to class on time and turn off cell phones and other devices that may distract others. In addition, students should pay full attention to whomever is speaking and refrain from interrupting others during a conversation. Finally, debate is encouraged, but it should be done in a courteous manner.

Academic integrity

Cheating and plagiarizing have no educational value. Our department expects all students to do their own work and give proper credit – using an accepted citation style – where credit is due.

Artificial intelligence

The use of generative AI in this class to create images, assets or complete writing assignments is prohibited. For this class, those uses are not considered “doing your own work” and constitute breaking the academic integrity policy.

AI can be used as a learning tool to help check errors and debug issues in your code. It can be a helpful tool, but should be used to help check and explain syntax, not create the syntax and code for you.

Writing

Good writing is an essential skill in all fields. All written work will be graded with an emphasis not only on content but also on mechanical accuracy.

Communication

Timely and effective communication with me and your classmates is essential for the duration of this course. Check your email frequently; more than once per day. Many course activities will be facilitated through your St. Thomas email account and unforeseen circumstances often impact class meetings, due dates, and assignments details. It is critical to your success in the class that you read and respond to these communiques as quickly as possible. I also expect you to contact me if you have to miss class, schedule a meeting during office hours, or discuss anything regarding your performance in the class. Finally, regularly review the course content on Canvas and on the companion course website (dima-456.github.io).

Grading

All professors in our department view an “A” grade as work that is truly exceptional. A “B” signifies very good, above-average work; and a “C” signifies competence with an appropriate grasp of the subject matter. A “D” is a passing grade despite deficiencies, and an “F” signifies failure.
